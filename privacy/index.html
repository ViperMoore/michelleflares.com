4. Why I Collect Your Data and the Legal Basis

Under UK GDPR, I must have a valid legal basis for processing your personal data. The legal bases I rely on are:

Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f) UK GDPR)

  • Responding to your enquiry and communicating with you about potential or ongoing work
  • Improving my website and services based on how visitors use them
  • Protecting my website and business from fraud or abuse

Contract performance (Article 6(1)(b) UK GDPR)

  • Processing your data when necessary to enter into or fulfil a contract with you — for example, to build your website, manage your hosting, or set up your email

Consent (Article 6(1)(a) UK GDPR)

  • If you tick the consent checkbox on the contact form, you are giving me consent to use your information to respond to your enquiry
  • You may withdraw your consent at any time by contacting me at hello@michelleflares.com

Legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c) UK GDPR)

  • Where I am required by law to retain certain records — for example, for tax and accounting purposes under HMRC rules

5. How I Use Your Data

I use your personal data only for the purposes for which it was collected. Specifically:

  • To respond to your enquiries and provide quotes for web design and related services
  • To deliver the services you have purchased — building your website, managing your hosting, setting up email, and providing ongoing care and support
  • To send you updates relating to your project or service (e.g. letting you know your website is live, or that your domain is due for renewal)
  • To process payments through Stripe
  • To maintain records for accounting and tax compliance
  • To improve the performance and usability of this website
  • To detect and prevent fraud or misuse of my services

I will never sell your personal data to third parties. I will never use your data for unsolicited marketing without your explicit consent.

7. How Long I Keep Your Data

I retain your personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes set out in this policy:

  • Enquiries that did not result in a project: I will delete your contact information within 12 months of our last communication, unless you have asked me to keep it for future reference.
  • Clients (active or completed projects): I retain project-related communications and records for 6 years after the end of our working relationship, in line with HMRC requirements for business records.
  • Payment records: Retained for 6 years from the date of transaction, as required by HMRC for tax purposes.
  • Analytics data: Google Analytics data is retained for 14 months by default (as configured in Google Analytics settings).
  • Website contact form submissions: Stored in my email inbox and deleted after 12 months if no project resulted.

When data is no longer needed, I delete it securely from my systems and request deletion from any relevant third-party processors where applicable.

8. How I Protect Your Data

I take the security of your personal data seriously and have implemented appropriate technical and organisational measures, including:

  • All data transmitted via this website is encrypted using SSL/TLS (HTTPS)
  • Payments are processed by Stripe, which holds PCI-DSS Level 1 certification — the highest level of payment security
  • Access to my email and business accounts is protected by strong passwords and two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • I do not store your payment card details — ever
  • I use reputable, security-maintained third-party services (Formspree, GitHub Pages, Stripe)
  • I only retain data for as long as necessary (see Section 7)

However, please be aware that no method of internet transmission is 100% secure. While I strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your data, I cannot guarantee absolute security.

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, I will notify the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) within 72 hours as required by UK GDPR, and I will notify you directly where required.

12. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

Under the UK General Data Protection Regulation and the Data Protection Act 2018, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

Right of access

You have the right to request a copy of the personal data I hold about you (a Subject Access Request). I will respond within one month of receiving your request.

Right to rectification

You have the right to request that I correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal data I hold about you.

Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")

You have the right to request that I delete your personal data in certain circumstances — for example, if the data is no longer necessary for the purpose it was collected, or if you withdraw consent. Note that I may need to retain certain data to comply with legal obligations (e.g. tax records).

Right to restrict processing

You have the right to request that I restrict the processing of your data in certain circumstances — for example, if you contest its accuracy or object to processing.

Right to data portability

Where processing is based on your consent or a contract, and is carried out by automated means, you have the right to receive your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format, and to request that I transmit it to another controller where technically feasible.

Right to object

You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data where I rely on legitimate interests as the legal basis. I will cease processing unless I can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests.

Rights related to automated decision-making

I do not use automated decision-making or profiling that produces legal or similarly significant effects on you. No decisions about you are made solely by automated means.

Right to withdraw consent

Where processing is based on consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.
